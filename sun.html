Sun

Composition: The Sun is primarily composed of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%), with trace amounts of oxygen, carbon, iron, and other elements.

Temperature: The core temperature reaches approximately 15 million degrees Celsius, while the surface temperature is around 5,500 degrees Celsius.

Structure: The Sun consists of several layers: the core, radiative zone, convective zone, and the corona, which is the outermost layer and has extremely high temperatures.
